Home Shopping

Consolidated existing tooling into a net-new dashboard to better support Orchard’s internal agents in providing a great customer experience to clients who are currently looking to buy a home.

Background

Orchard is a real estate startup that offers streamlined services and tooling for consumers looking to buy and/or sell their home. Orchard employs its own real estate agents, internally called “Home Advisors,” who support customers throughout their home shopping & buying experience.

Why invest in Home Shopping tooling?

  • Home Advisors play an integral role in customer acquisition because they are responsible for helping our customers find and close on their new homes. It’s important that they have access to great tooling to support them in managing a large pipeline of customers and in delivering a best-in-class customer experience.
  • HAs are one of our largest and most expensive teams, so there is a lot of value in driving efficiency for them. Currently, HAs are managing the home shopping process across a number of different systems, so consolidating that work into a single tool will help drive efficiency.
  • Before this project, HAs were using Salesforce to manage their home shoppers. Transitioning them out of Salesforce and into Deal Hub, our homegrown internal platform, will save us thousands on licensing costs.

Problem statement

How might we improve home shopping management tools so Home Advisors can more effectively serve their customers?

The project

Primary users

  • Home Advisors (HAs) are licensed real estate agents who work on the “buy side” of a transaction. Unlike a traditional agent, Orchard HAs have a large pipeline of customers and may be working with 10-20 customers at a time.
  • HAs spend 20-30% time out in the field meeting with customers for home tours and closings and the rest of their time writing contracts, checking in with customers, and other misc. admin tasks.

Research + defining scope

My PM and I kicked off this project by building an understanding of the current state of Deal Hub with current users to understand existing functionality and usability gaps.

To ensure we were building the right product and gain buy-in with HAs, we decided to run a 2-month beta program with 3 high-performing HAs and 1 of their managers to ensure a smooth transition. Throughout the program, we:

  • Ran card-sorting exercises with HAs to understand what information HAs need and want during the home shopping phase.
  • Concept-tested an initial design based on the high-level functionality requirements.
  • Beta group participants fully adopted DH and provided feedback on a weekly basis while eng finished out the next set of MVP+ features.

Project scope + requirements

I worked closely with my PM and our Brokerage team lead to determine the high-level requirements for this project while factoring in the user needs and pain points we'd uncovered. At a high level, HAs would be moving from using primarily Salesforce to Deal Hub, where we would need to support:

  • Managing a pipeline of all home shoppers
  • Viewing an activity feed of customer <> Orchard communications
  • Updating information about customer engagement and motivations (e.g. reason for moving)
  • Accessing customer’s saved homes & searches (via the Orchard customer dashboard)
  • Viewing home tour feedback and upcoming tours
  • Easily creating and checking off tasks

Outcome

The new dashboard + related work was rolled out smoothly & on-time. We ran a survey to understand HA satisfaction a few weeks after release, and found that most HAs feel that Deal Hub has made doing their jobs easier.

  • Overall tooling satisfaction took a small dip (-5%) - likely due to the transition in general and having to learn a new tool.
  • Satisfaction with pipeline management, tasking, and communication remained flat.
  • Satisfaction with managing new home closings increased (+26%).
  • When asked if Deal Hub has made doing their jobs easier, 57% said yes, 29% were neutral, and 14% disagreed.

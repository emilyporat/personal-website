What I Do

I do a little bit of everything, but I am most drawn towards the synthesis & organizational elements of UX. I like making sense of things. I like spreadsheets. My fridge is color-coded (jk, but you get the picture).

In practice, this applies to a couple different areas. First, I love translating raw data into actionable insights and solutions, and I can do that best when I'm most involved in the early research stages and initial design + strategy stages of a project. I also enjoy the other end of the spectrum - working with design systems and organizing component libraries. I've found that my type-A tendencies tend to complement my more right-brained colleagues in these areas.

My Principles

Think beyond pixels

I'm not just a pixel-pusher, professional rectangle-drawer, or a Sophisticated Executive of Shapes. I care about nailing down the "why" before the "how," and I'm not afraid to stand up for what I believe in.

Stay humble & keep learning

My formal education at Carnegie Mellon was in Information Systems and Human-Computer Interaction, but I didn't stop learning when I graduated. Reading books like Ruined by Design, The Best Interface is No Interface, and Qualitative Research and Evaluation (just to name a few) have helped me fill in the gaps and transition my career from case study to reality. They've influenced me to question my assumptions, ask 'why' more, and practice my craft with rigor.

Own your career

I don't wait around for the things I want — I got after them proactively. For example, when I noticed a major gap in Deloitte's new-hire experience, I took initiative to create a mentorship program for aspiring designers.

Teamwork makes the dream work

Design is a team sport, and part of working smart, not hard, is being able to effectively delegate. I know when to step up and take charge, but I also strive to give others opportunities to make an impact.

What I'm All About

My design process isn't an exact science, but it is scientific: I view every design concept as a hypothesis waiting to be tested. Spreadsheets and pro-con lists are my secret weapons when it comes to organizing and synthesizing complexity - which is what I do best.

While I consider myself a generalist, I shine most in the early stages of design because I firmly believe that (just enough) research is essential to getting a product right. There is nothing more satisfying to me than a neatly packaged deck that breaks down thousands of research findings into just a few slides.

I have really high standards for myself - I'm not physically capable of half-assing something. I know when to step up and take charge, but I also strive to give others opportunities to make an impact.

What I'm Not All About

I'm not a pixel-pusher, a professional rectangle-drawer, or a unicorn/ninja/superhero/insert-your-fave-buzzword. I care about nailing down the "why" before the "how," and I'm not afraid to stand up for what I believe in.

How I Got Here

I didn't stop learning when I graduated from CMU. Reading books like Ruined by Design, The Best Interface is No Interface, and Qualitative Research & Evaluation (just to name a few) have helped me fill in the gaps and transition my career from case study to reality. They've influenced me to question my assumptions, ask 'why' more, and practice my craft with rigor.

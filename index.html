UX Researcher & Designer // Washington DC

Emily Porat

I'm a pragmatist who knows how to work within constraints and through complexity to get the job done, well. I'm currently at Deloitte Digital, where my work has supported gov programs for those seeking food security, affordable housing, emergency management, and more.

What I'm All About

I prioritize utility, inclusivity, and usability (in that order).

My design process isn't an exact science, but it is scientific: I view every design concept as a hypothesis waiting to be tested. Spreadsheets and pro-con lists are my secret weapon when it comes to organizing and synthesizing complexity - which is what I do best.

While I consider myself a generalist, I shine most in the early stages of design because I firmly believe that (just enough) research is essential to getting a product right. There is nothing more satisfying to me than a neatly packaged deck that breaks down thousands of research findings into just a few slides.

I have really high standards for myself - I'm not physically capable of half-assing something. I know when to step up and take charge, but I also strive to give others opportunities to make an impact.

For more background on skills + experience, check out the about page or download my résumé

What I'm Not All About

I'm not a pixel-pusher, a professional rectangle-drawer, or a unicorn/ninja/superhero/insert-your-fave-buzzword. I care about nailing down the "why" before the "how," and I'm not afraid to stand up for what I believe in.

How I Got Here

My formal education at Carnegie Mellon was in Information Systems and Human-Computer Interaction, but I didn't stop learning when I graduated. Reading books from folks like Mike Monteiro, Golden Krishna, and Michael Patton (just to name a few) have helped me fill in the gaps and transition my career from case study to reality. They've influenced me to question my assumptions, ask 'why' more, and practice my craft with rigor.

This is a portfolio, after all.

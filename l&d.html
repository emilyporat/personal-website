LEARNING & DEVELOPMENT PORTAL
Summer 2018 | Deloitte Digital Summer Internship
Context
During my summer at Deloitte Digital as a UX Design Intern, I was tasked with redesigning the Learning and Development Portal: a website through which Deloitte Digital practitioners can request to attend external continuing education (CED) opportunities such as conferences and trainings.

I had full ownership over this project: I created my own project plan and timeline, I decided what methods and tools I wanted to use and learn, and I headed all the group efforts that went into the project.

Team
The Learning team is made up of three others: a product manager, project manager, and senior manager.
Skills
Heuristic Evaluations, Interviewing, Affinity Mapping, User testing, Wireframing, Prototyping, Presenting
Tools
Adobe InDesign, Sketch, PowerPoint, A lot of sticky notes
PROCESS
I followed the classic double-diamond process for this 8-week project, splitting each phase into roughly a two-week sprint. In the 'Discover' phase, I did a Heuristic Evaluation of the existing portal and conducted 20 interviews and 2 usability tests with users and stakeholders. In 'Define,' I worked with a team to create an Affinity Map from nearly 400 statements gathered from research. We also completed a project visioning session, and I created personas and user stories. I kicked off 'Develop' with a Crazy 8s session, and eventually worked through several iterations of wireframes and prototypes. In 'Deploy,' I created a style guide and began to work through visual designs for screens.
Heuristic Evaluation
My first step in understanding the existing portal was to evaluate it based on Nielsen's ten heuristics. Going through this process helped me pick apart major issues with the site and allowed me to familiarize myself with its features so I was better prepared going into interviews.     I didn't want to spend too much time testing the existing system because I anticipated completely starting from scratch, so I only did usability testing with two people. I also created a script in preparation, and what I observed helped me gain a better understanding of some of the pain points.

    Affinity Mapping
    After organizing and printing out 381 slips of paper with insights and interview quotes, we sorted them into "Level 3" groupings - the most specific supporting details. Initially, we used a word or phrase to describe each grouping, but by the end of this session, we had converted each phrase into an I-statement.
    Next, we organized the groupings into higher level I-statements (Level 2). Finally, we organized the Level 2 statements into Level 1 categories - one word that encompasses everything beneath it.
    Once we had finalized the groupings, we invited everyone in Deloitte Digital to "Walk the Wall," an exercise through which stakeholders review the finished diagram and leave notes that fall under three categories - key insights, questions, and design ideas.
